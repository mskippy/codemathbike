1.3 Parts of a Computer

ICT 8 • Unit 1 › Lesson 1.3

Lesson Outline

  • Warm-up — compare a 1990s PC with a modern gaming PC; submit answers to Forms and view a live word cloud
  • Activity A: Parts of Our Computer — label the diagram of the actual classroom computer (PPTX)
  • Activity B: Mystery Match — close-up photos of hardware parts, make your guesses, then check answers
  • Wrap-up — quick share: which computer part matters most to you and why?

Warm-up (5 min)

Your task:

  • Type two big differences you notice between these two computers.
  • Which one do you think is faster? Why?

Submit your answer

Activity A — Identifying the Parts of Our Computer (15 min)

We’ll learn about the real computers in our classroom by labeling the parts on the image below.

Steps
  1. Download 1.3 Parts of My Computer.pptx . Don’t forget to rename and save it in the right folder!
  2. Drag the labels (Power Button, Audio Output, USB, USB-C, etc.) onto the correct places on the computer image.
  3. Create new labels for any other parts you can identify.
  4. Identify the parts on the back of your tower.

Need help identifying the parts of a computer? Try this tutorial.

Activity B — Mystery Match (20 min)

Can you recognize computer parts just from a close-up photo? Write down your guesses, then check your answers after the reveal.

Steps
  1. Download and open the 1.3 Myster Match slides.
  2. For each close-up photo, write in the notes section of the slides:
    “I think this is ______ because ______.”
  3. Add a one-sentence note about what the part does.
Parts you will see
  • RAM stick
  • SSD (solid state drive)
  • CPU fan / heatsink
  • GPU (graphics card)
  • Power connectors
  • Ports (USB, HDMI, Ethernet, etc.)

Wrap-up (10 min)

Let’s finish by reflecting on what was learned in this lesson.

Discussion Prompts
  • Which computer part is most important to you and why?
  • If you could upgrade only one part of a computer, which would it be?

Answer these prompts in Teams.
